**Himkok: Comprehensive Brand Analysis**

**1\. Brand Identity Analysis**

\*\*Core Concept and V \([Crypto Cocktails Have Arrived | PUNCH](https://punchdrink.com/articles/crypto-nft-cocktails-have-arrived/#:~:text=But%20bartenders%20are%20taking%20a,creative%20works%20like%20art%20or)\)k \(pronounced *Him\-kook*, meaning “moonshine”\) was founded in 2015 with a mission to **embody Norwegian culture through cocktails and spirits**\. From day one, the bar\-distillery has championed values of authenticity, local craftsmanship, and innovation\. *“Staying true to the surrounding nature and local accessibility,”* Himkok creates drinks with an *“authentic Nordic taste”* using sustainable methods\. This encapsulates core values of **localism** \(using regional ingredients\), **heritage** \(drawing on Norwegian traditions\), **sustainability**, and **experimentation**\. The name itself – a colloquial term for homemade liquor – reinforces a down\-to\-earth, craft ethos\. As one review notes, \*“When you realise that Himkok translates to moonshine\.\.\. everything in this temple of drinks makes \([The Scandi factor: how Himkok is leading the charge of the Nordic \.\.\.](https://www.theworlds50best.com/stories/News/himkok-highest-climber-worlds-50-best-bars-2023.html#:~:text=When%2050%20Best%20introduced%20the,judging%20criteria%20with%20flying)\) \([Himkok announces aquavit collaboration with Linie \- Drinks International \- The global choice for drinks buyers](https://drinksint.com/news/fullstory.php/aid/10942/Himkok_announces_aquavit_collaboration_with_Linie.html#:~:text=Oslo,its%20limited%20edition%20Himkok%20Linie)\) concept is a showcase of Norway from distillery to glass\.

**Evolution of Visual Identity:** Himkok’s branding weaves together historical and cultural motifs\. The bar is housed in an 1800s building that was once a pharmacy warehouse, and this legacy shapes its look and feel\. Bartenders famously wear **white lab coats** as a nod to pharmacists \(who played a role in early cocktail history\), and the **logo and bottles echo 19th\-century apothecary designs**\. The visual identity, created by Oslo design firm Olssøn Barbieri in 2019, prominently features a **snake symbol**\. Dubbed the “moonshine bite,” the snake motif is inspired by the **Huggorm** \(Vipera berus\), Norway’s only venomous snake\. This symbol carries rich meaning in Scandinavian folklore – connecting to themes of medicine, alchemy, wisdom and eternity – which aligns with Himkok’s fusion of old tradition and modern technique\. The **typography** similarly blends old and new; for example, the bar uses a vintage\-style sans serif \(Monokrom’s *Telefon* typeface\) that evokes old phone directories, reinforcing a retro\-industrial vibe\. Over time, Himkok’s color palette and imagery have stayed understated and **functional \(black, white, steel, wood\)**, consistent with a speakeasy&#x2F;laboratory aesthetic\. Every touchpoint – from the embossed **“H” logo on the ice cubes** in cocktails to the custom slim bottles – tells a coherent story of Norwegian craft meeting scientific precision\.

*Himkok’s main bar exudes a speakeasy\-meets\-laboratory vibe, with on\-site distilling equipment integrated into the décor\. The back bar displays glass holding tanks and an “H” logo motif, reflecting the brand’s fusion of tradition and innovation\.*

**Storytelling and Norwegian Cultural References:** Across all brand touchpoints, Himkok consistently highlights Norwegian culture\. The **ingredients and flavors** are hyper\-local – each cocktail is built around a Norwegian produce item or traditional delicacy – and menus often pay homage to classic Norwegian foods \(e\.g\. desserts like *multekrem* cloudberry cream\)\. The bar even grows its own herbs in a greenhouse and distills national spirits \(aquavit, gin\) on\-site, making over **80% of ingredients in\-house**\. Interior elements like **wooden fixtures** and the hidden courtyard evoke Oslo’s history, while touches like a functioning **barbershop on the premises** give a playful nod to local bar culture \(a surprise amenity noted by visitors\)\. Himkok’s very layout – with a cider bar, a cocktail lounge, and a taptail \(tap cocktail\) bar on different floors – is designed to be an *“endless warren”* of experiences showcasing facets of Norwegian drinking culture\. The brand name *Himkok* itself influences perception: it positions the bar as proudly unpretentious and rooted in Norwegian moonshining heritage, which helps set it apart from generic upscale “international” cocktail bars\. This story of **local heritage meets modern mastery** is reinforced in all brand communications and experiences\. As the team describes, Himkok is a *“living embodiment of Norwegian craftsmanship, innovation, and pride,”* a place that stays true to its roots while embracing modernity\. This identity resonates strongly; for example, LINIE Aquavit’s manager noted *“Himkok has shown that they have the ability to capture the essence of Norwegian culture and traditions and transform it into a modern setting”*\.

**Multi\-Faceted Brand Roles:** Unusually, Himkok operates as four brands in one – **bar, distillery, ready\-to\-drink \(RTD\) producer, and consultancy** – yet maintains a cohesive identity throughout\. The in\-house **distillery** is not just a back\-end feature but a core part of the brand story: it produces Himkok’s own aquavit, gin and vodka which flow directly into the cocktails, exemplifying farm\-to\-glass \(or rather, *still\-to\-glass*\) authenticity\. The distillery’s presence is central to the bar’s concept, underscoring innovation and making Himkok literally *“the world’s first distillery bar”* when it opened\. Meanwhile, the **RTD line** \(bottled or canned cocktails\) is an extension of the bar’s philosophy beyond the venue – these products, developed and **scaled in partnership with Aass Brewery**, carry the same Nordic ingredients and quality as the on\-site drinks\. \(For example, Himkok’s bottled Oslo Mule and Oslo Paloma won gold in The Spirits Business Pre\-Mixed Awards\.\) Rather than diluting the brand, the RTDs reinforce Himkok’s experimental and entrepreneurial spirit\. The **consultancy arm** likewise leverages Himkok’s identity as a top\-ranked bar to help other establishments, emphasizing custom solutions, creativity and excellence\. In all these facets, the brand maintains a balance: the core Himkok name&#x2F;ethos is always front and center \(e\.g\. the RTD line uses the Himkok name and iconography, and consultancy explicitly trades on Himkok’s reputation\)\. This cohesive branding allows Himkok to be a bar, a product, and a service brand at once – all united by the same values of Norwegian flavor, quality and innovation\. In short, whether you encounter Himkok in person in Oslo or via a bottled cocktail abroad, you get a consistent story of *“moonshine” tradition elevated to world\-class status\.*

**2\. Communications Strategy**

**Channels and Audience Segmentation:** Given Norway’s strict regulations on alcohol advertising, Himkok has crafted a clever communications strategy that relies on experiential marketing, PR, and digital content rather than overt ads\. The bar communicates through **owned channels** like its website and social media \(Instagram and Facebook\) and through rich **earned media** coverage in press and industry publications\. Each channel targets different segments:

- **Local Audiences:** Locally, Himkok positions itself through word\-of\-mouth and community presence\. The bar hosts industry events, educational courses, and engages in initiatives that naturally attract Norwegian bar\-goers without “advertising” per se\. For example, Himkok offers **cocktail courses** and even “hire a bartender” services, which serve as both revenue streams and subtle marketing to locals \(turning guests and clients into brand ambassadors\)\. The tone for locals is often informative and prideful – highlighting Norwegian ingredients and cultural elements that natives appreciate\. Himkok also collaborates with local cultural institutions \(like the Munch Museum fashion show, see Section 5\) which garners attention within Oslo’s creative community\. By focusing on being a must\-visit *experience* \(e\.g\. a hidden bar with multiple concepts under one roof\), Himkok ensures locals talk about it organically on forums and social platforms\. Indeed, the bar became *“the talk of the town”* soon after opening by inviting locals to rethink cocktails in a beer\-dominated city\.
- **International Audiences:** Globally, Himkok leans heavily on its **reputation in rankings and media** to communicate its brand\. The team strategically engages with the **World’s 50 Best Bars** platform and tales\-of\-the\-cocktail\-type press\. This has paid off with frequent features in international outlets from *Campari Academy* to *Class Magazine*, building an image of Himkok as a pilgrimage site for cocktail enthusiasts\. The messaging for international audiences underscores uniqueness \(e\.g\. *“part bar, part distillery, part lab”* concept\) and Scandinavian cool\. Himkok’s team does guest shifts and pop\-ups abroad, effectively using **face\-to\-face events** to communicate the brand in key markets \(Asia, US, Europe\) without traditional ads\. Social media content is also slightly tailored: their Instagram \(over 30k followers\) alternates between Norwegian\-language posts \(for locals\) and English posts \(for global fans\), carefully navigating the line between engaging followers and not overtly “promoting” alcohol in violation of local laws\.

**Tone of Voice and Messaging:** Across channels, Himkok maintains an **educational yet inviting tone**\. They often share the *“why”* behind each cocktail or initiative, reinforcing the brand story\. For instance, an Instagram post highlights *“our cocktail menu is a showpiece for Norwegian culture and its flavours\.\.\. we work seasonally with local producers”* – a message that is both promotional and informational, delivered in a matter\-of\-fact voice\. The tone is generally **proud but humble**: Himkok emphasizes being Norwegian\-first \(pride in local culture\) but also portrays itself as a continuous experimenter \(humility to keep innovating\)\. In media interviews, the bar team \(like bar manager Maroš Dzurus\) often expresses gratitude and a sense of being “humbled” by recognition, which humanizes the brand despite its fame\. The messaging framework typically centers on a few pillars – **Norwegian produce**, **sustainability**, **innovation**, and **hospitality excellence** – and nearly every piece of content ties back to these\. Even when launching something novel like an NFT cocktail menu, Himkok framed it around supporting the community and pushing industry boundaries\. This consistent messaging builds trust that Himkok’s communications are not just marketing fluff but genuine reflections of their practices\.

**Navigating Advertising Restrictions:** Norway’s alcohol marketing ban means Himkok cannot run ads or happy\-hour promos\. Instead, they have become adept at **PR and storytelling**\. The bar leverages its many awards as de facto marketing – for example, press releases and website updates tout honors like “ranked No\.11 in the world”, which informs the public in a news\-like manner rather than an advert\. Media coverage is a critical part of their strategy: by cultivating relationships with journalists and inviting international juries, Himkok ensures a steady stream of articles that act as *indirect advertising*\. Additionally, Himkok focuses on **content marketing**: their menu launches are turned into newsworthy events \(like the Sony Music collaboration menu launch\) that publications eagerly cover, giving Himkok publicity within the bounds of the law\. On social media, they avoid direct encouragements to drink; instead, they showcase the artistry of cocktails, the ambience of the venue, and behind\-the\-scenes looks at R&amp;D\. This sparks interest without overtly selling alcohol\. In one instance, Himkok’s team even used **NFTs** as a novel communication method – essentially turning a menu into a digital art fundraiser – which garnered press in both tech and drinks circles\. By being on the forefront of such trends, Himkok generates buzz that circumvents traditional ads\.

**Highlighting Sustainability Authentically:** A cornerstone of Himkok’s communications is sustainability, but it’s always presented as a natural part of the brand rather than a forced marketing angle\. For example, when the bar won the inaugural *Sustainable Bar Award* in 2018, it was because the judges saw genuine practices \(local sourcing, seasonal menus, reduced waste\)\. In communications, Himkok details these efforts: they might mention using an **eco\-friendly custom bottle** \(lightweight and recyclable\) for their spirits, or the fact that garnishes and ingredients come from nearby farms and even their own urban garden\. By providing specifics, the bar’s messaging avoids “greenwashing” and instead shares concrete steps – e\.g\. noting that *over 80% of their ingredients are made or distilled in\-house* which cuts supply chain footprint, or that their bottle corks are eco\-polymer and inks organic\. This factual, low\-key approach in communications \(often delivered in interviews or on their website\) reinforces Himkok’s credibility\. They also engage in **industry panels on sustainability**: during their 10th anniversary, they hosted roundtable discussions on *“innovation, sustainability, and longevity in the bar world”* led by an expert journalist, aligning their brand with thought leadership in this space\. Such actions are then communicated via press releases and social posts, showing that Himkok doesn’t just talk the talk but actively participates in advancing sustainable practices\.

**Social Media Content &amp; Engagement:** On platforms like Instagram, Himkok’s content strategy focuses on **storytelling and engagement**\. Posts often feature beautiful cocktail photos, behind\-the\-bar shots, and team highlights, usually accompanied by captions crediting local ingredients or partners\. The bar encourages engagement by invoking Norwegian nostalgia \(for example, a post about a cocktail inspired by Solo soda and Kvikk Lunsj chocolate might ask followers about their favorite childhood treat\)\. User\-generated content is regularly reshared, effectively turning guests into brand messengers\. Despite its fame, Himkok keeps a personable tone in replies, thanking visitors who tag them and answering questions \(e\.g\. about how to book, or what tonight’s menu is\)\. Another strategy is leveraging **influencer and community networks**: they collaborate with cocktail influencers and tourism boards \(without overt sponsorship language\) – for instance, features on travel blogs and appearances in cocktail festival livestreams – to maintain a broad online presence\. In comparison to other top bars, Himkok’s digital presence is notably robust\. In fact, in 2024 an independent index \(*Top 500 Bars*\) named Himkok the **“world’s most influential bar” online**, based on its visibility and impact across media and social platforms\. This indicates that their communications strategy – relying on rich content and constant engagement instead of traditional ads – has successfully built a strong global brand following\.

**Local vs\. International Messaging Tactics:** While the overarching story stays the same, Himkok does tailor certain messages for local versus international audiences\. Locally, there is an air of **community and national pride** – for example, hosting Norwegian\-language discussions about aquavit or partnering with Norwegian artists which gets coverage in national media\. Internationally, the messaging is more about **novelty and excellence** – emphasizing the awards and the fact that it’s a unique destination bar\. For instance, a UK or US press article might headline Himkok as *“Oslo’s award\-winning speakeasy\-distillery that’s redefining Nordic cocktails”*, which intrigues readers as something exotic\. Local media, on the other hand, might highlight how Himkok is preserving Norwegian drinking traditions\. Himkok manages this dual messaging well\. They provide press materials in English for global outlets \(ensuring accurate storytelling abroad\) while also engaging with Norwegian press in the native language for authenticity at home\. Even on their website, the content is available in English but the nuances of certain terms \(like explanations of cultural ingredients\) show an intent to educate international visitors without alienating locals\. Ultimately, by balancing local intimacy with international prestige, Himkok sustains a positive image on both fronts\.

**Public Relations and Media Coverage:** Proactive PR has been key to Himkok’s rise\. The brand regularly issues press releases for major milestones \(menu launches, anniversary events, collaborations, awards\) which are picked up by spirits websites and news outlets\. They have also welcomed media into their space – from global ranking voters to documentarians – turning the bar into a media\-friendly subject\. This approach has landed Himkok features in **The World’s 50 Best Bars stories section**, *The Spirits Business*, *Punch*, *Drinks International*, etc\., often highlighting exactly the brand points they want to convey \(sustainability, innovation, cultural tie\-ins\)\. For example, a *50 Best* news piece in 2023 focused on Himkok leading the “Nordic bar uprising” and detailed their philosophy of elevating Norwegian produce – essentially PR gold that reads as editorial\. Himkok’s team members also actively participate in **industry talks and competitions**, which generates further press\. Maroš Dzurus giving interviews about their menu concept or owner Erk Potur speaking on a panel automatically turns into coverage for Himkok\. Additionally, the bar smartly leverages its **awards in marketing** \(see next section\): whenever they climb in the rankings or win a prize, that news is amplified through PR channels\. The result is a steady drumbeat of positive media narratives that keep Himkok top\-of\-mind globally, all achieved without direct advertising\.

**3\. Industry Recognition &amp; Awards**

Himkok has accumulated an impressive list of awards and accolades, firmly establishing its reputation\. Below is a **timeline of Himkok’s rankings on “The World’s 50 Best Bars” list and related honors**:

- **2016:** Debuted on **The World’s 50 Best Bars** at **No\. 42** just one year after opening\. This immediate recognition signaled Himkok’s impact as a newcomer\.
- **2017:** Climbed to **No\. 20** in the world\. This jump into the top 20 reflected growing international esteem\. \(Notably, Himkok was also named **Best Bar in Norway** by regional experts around this time, as evidenced by local awards nominations\.\)
- **2018:** Ranked **No\. 19** globally\. Himkok also won the inaugural **“Sustainable Bar Award” \(2018\)**, recognizing its innovative environmental and community practices\. It was highlighted that Himkok fulfilled the sustainability criteria *“with flying colours,”* being chosen as the very first recipient of this honor\.
- **2019:** Ranked **No\. 17** – its highest position in the 2010s\. At the 2019 ceremony, Himkok’s achievements in showcasing Norwegian flavors on a world stage were celebrated\. This year they were also nominated as a Top 10 finalist for **“Best International Bar”** at the Tales of the Cocktail *Spirited Awards* \(North America’s prestigious bar awards\), underlining peer recognition beyond the 50 Best list\.
- **2020:** Ranked **No\. 30** on World’s 50 Best\. The slight drop was likely due in part to the pandemic era disruptions\. Nonetheless, staying on the list demonstrated resilience\. \(In 2020, Himkok also launched its award\-winning RTD line, earning **Gold medals** in The Spirits Business pre\-mixed cocktails awards for two recipes\.\)
- **2021:** Himkok temporarily fell out of the Top 50 \(it was listed at **\#79 in the extended 51–100 list**\)\. This off\-year could be attributed to pandemic lockdowns limiting international visits\. However, the bar rebounded strongly…
- **2022:** Re\-entered **The World’s 50 Best Bars** at **No\. 43** \(marked as a “Re\-Entry”\)\. This comeback was noted by the organizers as Himkok continuing to push boundaries\. Indeed, industry watchers saw 2022 as a renaissance for Himkok – the bar also won **“Best Cocktail Menu” in Norway** at the 2022 Bartenders’ Choice Awards \(a respected Nordic industry award\) for its innovative menu that year\.
- **2023:** Achieved its **highest ranking to date at No\. 10** in the world\. This 33\-place leap from the previous year earned Himkok the **“Nikka Highest Climber Award 2023”** for the biggest rank jump of any bar\. It marked Himkok’s seventh appearance on the 50 Best list and solidified its status as a global cocktail destination\. *“We’re still processing that we made it into the top 10,”* said bar manager Maroš Dzurus upon the achievement, attributing it to a long\-term focus on showcasing *“the very best of Norwegian hospitality”*\.
- **2024:** Ranked **No\. 11** in The World’s 50 Best Bars\. While just outside the top ten, Himkok received a special honor in 2024: the **“Bareksten Best Bar Design Award”** for its outstanding venue design and atmosphere\. This award recognized elements like Himkok’s multi\-space interior, the integration of distilling equipment into the bar, and its distinctive aesthetic\. With this, Himkok proved its excellence not only in drinks but also in overall concept and design\. By 2024, Himkok was one of only a few bars worldwide to consistently remain in or around the top 20 over a span of 8 years – a testament to enduring quality\.

**Other Major Awards and Recognitions:** Beyond the 50 Best Bars rankings, Himkok has amassed numerous accolades:

- At the **Bartenders’ Choice Awards \(Nordic\)**, Himkok has been a frequent honoree\. In addition to *Best Cocktail Menu 2022*, in 2024 one of its signature drinks \(“Birch”\) won **Best Signature Cocktail** in the Nordics, illustrating how individual creations from Himkok gain acclaim\. In 2022, Himkok again made the regional Top 10 Honorees list for Best International Bar\. In 2021, *Punch* magazine cited Himkok’s NFT cocktail menu as a pioneering concept, and the bar was invited to speak on this at industry webinars\. **Leveraging Awards in Marketing:** Himkok has expertly used these recognitions to further its brand\. The bar prominently lists its rankings and awards on its official site and marketing materials\. For example, the consultancy page opens by noting Himkok as *“ranked among the world’s top 50 bars,”* immediately establishing credibility for potential clients\. Each time the bar moves up in the 50 Best list, a flurry of media coverage and social media celebration follows – essentially providing free publicity\. Himkok’s team makes a point to attend award ceremonies \(often clad in their signature attire\), creating photo opportunities that circulate online\. After major wins, they often host local celebrations or guest shifts, which are communicated as special events \(drawing in patrons who want to share in the pride\)\. The **World’s 50 Best Bars logo and accolades** are displayed at the venue as well, which visitors often mention\. One TripAdvisor review from 2023 starts by saying *“One of world’s best 50 bars\!\.\.\. Best cocktails ever\!”* – showing how the 50 Best status becomes part of the customer perception and word\-of\-mouth\.

In marketing terms, each award has become a **trust signal** and a story to tell\. For instance, after winning the Sustainable Bar Award, Himkok doubled down on sharing what made it sustainable – turning an award into an educational marketing angle that differentiates them\. Similarly, being honored for design in 2024 gave them a reason to invite lifestyle and architecture media for a fresh look at the space\. Overall, Himkok doesn’t rest on laurels; it integrates them into its narrative \(e\.g\., *“award\-winning cocktails available bottled”* or *“from the team behind one of the World’s 50 Best Bars”* in consultancy pitches\)\. This leveraging extends to collaborations too – other brands want to partner with Himkok because of its accolades, leading to high\-profile joint projects \(which in turn create more publicity; a virtuous cycle\)\.

**Innovations Cited in Award Wins:** Many of Himkok’s awards have specifically cited the bar’s innovative practices\. The Sustainable Bar Award in 2018 highlighted how Himkok set a precedent by sourcing locally and reducing waste before it was trendy\. The Highest Climber Award in 2023 was essentially a nod to the new heights the bar achieved by reinventing its menu concept \(the “Beats &amp; Sips” menu with music integration\) and expanding its reach\. Judges and media often mention **unique features**: the on\-site distillery and piped spirits system, the multi\-room format \(with that hidden cider bar and barber shop\), and the tech\-forward menu experiences \(like scannable ingredient info and NFTs\)\. These innovations not only win awards but give other bars a template to follow\. For example, after Himkok’s success, more bars in Scandinavia began installing micro\-distilleries or highlighting hyper\-local ingredients, sparking what some call the *“Scandi factor”* in cocktail bars\. In essence, Himkok’s awards are frequently tied to the bar doing something first or doing it best – whether it’s sustainability, menu creativity, or immersive concept – which reinforces its image as an industry leader\.

**Business Impact of Awards:** The tangible business impacts of these recognitions have been significant\. Climbing the World’s 50 Best list correlates with spikes in patronage – especially international tourists who plan trips around visiting top\-ranked bars\. After making the top 20 and eventually top 10, Himkok saw a surge of visitors from abroad \(sometimes lining up outside for a chance to experience it\)\. Moreover, awards have opened new revenue streams: the RTD cocktails secured wider distribution through Aass Brewery *because* the brand was a proven winner \(Aass’s announcement proudly calls it the *“world famous cocktail bar Himkok”* when pitching the product line\)\. The consultancy branch likewise thrives on the bar’s award cachet – other venues hire Himkok’s experts in hopes of replicating some of that success\. Finally, being highly ranked provides a buffer in tough times; locals rallied to support Himkok during COVID\-related closures in part due to pride that *“our Oslo bar is among the world’s best\.”* This goodwill is priceless brand equity earned via accolades\.

**Comparison to Other Top Bars:** Compared to other global top bars, Himkok’s award profile is notable for its **breadth**\. Many bars might rank highly but have fewer diverse awards\. Himkok not only ranks consistently but has special awards for sustainability and design, menu awards regionally, and product awards – a wide spectrum\. Unlike some iconic bars that might peak at \#1 and then fall off, Himkok has shown steadiness and even resurgence, which suggests strong fundamentals\. Regionally, it stands out as the most awarded Nordic bar ever; for instance, Stockholm’s Tjoget and Copenhagen’s Ruby have made the 50 Best, but none have amassed the combination of global and local awards that Himkok has\. In the global context, its trajectory \(rapid rise, slight dip, then dramatic comeback\) is often compared to innovative bars like Singapore’s Native or London’s Lyaness, which similarly have won theme awards \(e\.g\., Sustainable Bar\) and then climbed into the top 10\. However, Himkok’s unique position as a **bar\-distillery** sets it apart – it competes with cocktail bars on one hand, and spirits producers on another \(winning awards in both domains\)\. This crossover success is relatively rare\. Overall, Himkok’s decorated profile places it in an elite league of bars that are not only **highly ranked but also highly regarded for specific strengths** – in Himkok’s case, its culture\-driven concept and innovative spirit\. This multi\-dimensional acclaim has cemented Himkok’s brand as **globally elite** yet **distinctly different** from any other bar on the list\.

**4\. Professional Networks &amp; Industry Influence**

From its early days, Himkok has plugged into professional networks and cultivated industry influence far beyond Oslo\. The bar is both a leader in the Scandinavian bar community and an active participant in the global cocktail scene\.

**Industry Affiliations and Associations:** Himkok and its team are well\-connected to various bartender guilds and associations\. The bar’s leadership and staff frequently engage with the **Norwegian Bartenders’ Guild** and have representation in organizations like the **Nordic Bar Syndicate** \(a collective of top Nordic bars sharing knowledge\)\. While not a formal franchise or chain, Himkok has become a hub in the Scandinavian cocktail network – regularly collaborating with other acclaimed Nordic venues\. For example, at their 10\-year anniversary event, they hosted a *“Scandinavian Bar Takeover”* night featuring top talent from Stockholm \(Röda Huset, Tjoget\) and Copenhagen \(Bird\)\. This reflects the respect Himkok commands among regional peers; it’s seen as an institution that can convene the best of the Nordics\. The bar also maintains close ties with **Norwegian distillers and breweries** \(through partnerships like those with LINIE and Aass\), which situates it at an intersection of the bar industry and the spirits production industry\.

On the international stage, Himkok has aligned with initiatives like **50 Best’s bartenders’ forums** and sustainability councils\. Key team members often appear at global industry conferences – for instance, bar manager Maroš Dzurus and head of R&amp;D Paul Aguilar have spoken at events like Bar Convent Berlin and Tales of the Cocktail seminars, sharing Himkok’s approach to local sourcing and menu innovation\. Such appearances enhance Himkok’s influence, as they contribute to shaping industry trends \(e\.g\. many attendees hearing about Himkok’s practices later implement similar ideas in their bars\)\.

**Key Personnel and Thought Leadership:** Several individuals from Himkok’s team have themselves become influential figures, which in turn elevates the brand’s network\. **Erk Potur**, Himkok’s founder\-owner, is regarded as a visionary in bar concept development\. He is often invited to judge cocktail competitions and consult on bar projects internationally \(outside of Himkok’s own consultancy service\)\. His presence on panels – for example, discussing bar entrepreneurship – spreads Himkok’s philosophy widely\. **Maroš Dzurus**, the longtime bar manager, is similarly prominent\. Being Slovakian\-born and having worked at Himkok since the beginning, Dzurus bridges communities; he’s delivered guest lectures in Central Europe and Asia about Nordic cocktail techniques\. In interviews, his eyes *“glow when asked about the opportunity to work this close”* to a distillery and Norwegian ingredients, and that passion is contagious\. He’s been featured by *Campari Academy* as a changemaker and by Drinks World magazine detailing Himkok’s journey\. Furthermore, **Monica Berg** \(mentioned earlier as an alum\) co\-founded P\(our\) – a nonprofit bartending think tank – and often cites her time at Himkok as formative in her understanding of sustainability and flavors\. This kind of testimonial by alumni in turn feeds back into Himkok’s standing among professionals: younger bartenders worldwide now seek to stage or work at Himkok to learn from “the source” of these ideas\.

The bar also sends its team to compete \(and often win\) in competitions, which increases their network\. For example, Himkok bartenders have participated in Diageo’s World Class competition; in 2020 one made it to the global finals, forging connections with top mixologists from 50\+ countries\. These competitions act like a network exchange – competitors later visit each other’s bars for guest shifts, etc\. Thus, a World Class or Bacardi Legacy experience by a Himkok staffer leads to reciprocal guest spots and friendships across continents\.

**Global Guest Shifts and Collaborations:** Himkok has been very active in the global “guest shift” circuit\. The bar regularly hosts visiting bartenders from other world\-famous bars and sends its own team members abroad for pop\-ups\. These exchanges have linked Himkok with bars in New York, London, Singapore, Dubai, and more\. For instance, in 2019 Maroš Dzurus did a guest shift at the Columbia Room in Washington D\.C\., turning it *“into a Nordic cocktail garden”* for one night\.
